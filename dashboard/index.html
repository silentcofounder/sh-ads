The Collapse — Root Cause Analysis

The sequence that destroyed 10 months of algorithm learning in 5 weeks (Oct → Dec 2025)
1
Nov 10: Consultant adds "Purchases" goal while sign-ups still primary Root Cause

The algorithm had spent 10 months learning to find sign-up converters. Adding a "Purchases" goal it had never seen before — with zero historical data — meant it suddenly had two conflicting objectives. Google's Smart Bidding couldn't reconcile them. Both campaigns entered deep learning mode simultaneously.

2
Nov 10: Location targeting tightened same day Compounding Factor

Switching from "people searching about Kenya" to "people physically in Kenya" was the right long-term fix but terrible timing. It dramatically reduced the eligible auction pool overnight — from global English speakers interested in Kenya to only Kenyans. Combined with the conversion goal change, Google was now bidding for a brand new audience with a brand new conversion event simultaneously.

3
Dec 17: Both campaigns paused same minute Kill Shot

Whatever fragile recovery the algorithm was making during November was completely wiped out when both campaigns were paused on Dec 17. Pausing a Smart Bidding campaign wipes its short-term learning. Reactivating in January meant starting from zero — with a budget that had been cut, a new location targeting setup, and still-conflicting conversion goals.

4
Jan 19: tROAS set at 800% on campaigns with zero purchase data Consultant Error

Setting an 800% Target ROAS means Google needs to return $8 for every $1 spent. With zero historical purchase conversion data and a budget of $5–9/day, this was mathematically impossible. Google responded by barely bidding at all — hence near-zero spend in Jan. Reducing to 300% on Jan 19 was the right correction but the damage from the combined changes was already done.
